# Twilio Launches PM Radar: Internal Product Intelligence Platform That Transforms Product Research from Quarterly Reactive Analysis to Daily Proactive Insights

**SAN FRANCISCO, March 31, 2026** — Twilio today launched PM Radar, an internal AI-powered platform that automates product intelligence gathering for Product Managers across the company. PM Radar continuously monitors industry trends, competitive moves, social conversations, and internal customer signals, delivering synthesized weekly intelligence digests that Product Managers use to prioritize roadmaps and respond to market shifts. Product Managers who previously spent 8-10 hours per week on manual research—or skipped research entirely between quarterly deep-dives—now receive comprehensive intelligence in a 30-minute Monday morning review, enabling proactive rather than reactive product strategy.

Twilio Product Managers faced an impossible tradeoff: spend 8-10 hours every week manually tracking competitors, reading industry reports, monitoring Reddit discussions, and synthesizing Zendesk tickets and Gong calls—or accept being reactive instead of proactive. The reality for most PMs became quarterly or bi-annual research sprints when bandwidth allowed, leaving them blind to competitive launches, emerging fraud patterns, and customer sentiment shifts for weeks or months at a time. SD, a Product Manager for Twilio's messaging fraud and risk prevention products, discovered competitor fraud detection launches 2-3 weeks after they shipped, learned about new SMS pumping attack patterns from customer escalations rather than proactive monitoring, and watched Reddit discussions about Twilio SMS pumping spike 300% without any systematic way to track them. Customer feedback remained fragmented across Zendesk support tickets, Slack VOC channels, Gong sales call transcripts, and G2 reviews, with no single view to identify patterns or correlations. The impact rippled through product strategy: roadmap decisions relied on incomplete information, competitive threats went undetected until customers raised objections in sales calls, and market opportunities appeared only after competitors had already moved.

PM Radar eliminates the manual research burden through automated multi-source intelligence gathering and AI-powered synthesis. Each Product Manager configures their own workspace in 10 minutes, selecting their product area, competitors to monitor, industry sources to track, and internal data connections (Zendesk tags, Slack channels, Gong call filters). Every Monday morning at 9am, PMs receive a comprehensive weekly digest summarizing industry trends from 15+ RSS feeds, competitive intelligence from automated website monitoring and change detection, social listening insights from Reddit and other platforms, and internal customer signals aggregated from support tickets, sales calls, and VOC channels. When high-priority anomalies are detected—such as three competitors launching similar features within 48 hours, or Reddit sentiment spiking 300%—autonomous investigation agents automatically deep-dive overnight, scraping competitor product pages, searching Gong call transcripts for related customer conversations, and cross-referencing Slack history to deliver a detailed investigation memo by Monday morning. Product Managers now detect competitive launches within days instead of weeks, identify emerging fraud patterns from industry reports before they impact customers, and correlate Reddit discussions with internal support trends to prioritize backlog items based on actual market signals rather than gut feel.

> *"Last quarter, I missed a major competitor fraud detection launch for three weeks because I was heads-down shipping our own features," said SD, Product Manager for Twilio Messaging Fraud Prevention. "This quarter, PM Radar flagged three competitor launches on Monday morning with a full investigation memo analyzing their features, our gaps, and which customers had already asked us for similar capabilities. I walked into our roadmap planning meeting with complete competitive context instead of scrambling to research after the fact."*

For Twilio Product Managers, getting started with PM Radar requires no installation or training. Simply visit the internal PM Radar portal, create a workspace for your product area, add 2-5 competitors you want to monitor, select from a curated list of industry RSS feeds or add your own custom sources, connect your Zendesk tags and Slack VOC channels, and configure your Monday morning digest delivery time. The platform handles the rest: automated daily collection from all configured sources, AI-powered analysis detecting patterns and anomalies, autonomous investigation when high-priority signals trigger, and synthesized weekly intelligence delivered via email with attachments for detailed investigation memos. PMs use Monday morning insights to groom their backlog, reprioritizing features based on most frequent customer concerns surfaced from Zendesk and Gong, adding new items to address evolving fraud patterns identified in industry research, and cross-referencing competitive moves against Twilio's internal product databases to identify strategic gaps.

> *"PM Radar represents a fundamental shift in how Twilio Product Managers stay informed about their markets," said Chris Boran, VP of Platform Engineering at Twilio. "Our PMs were making multi-million dollar roadmap decisions based on quarterly research sprints and anecdotal customer conversations. Now they have always-on intelligence that synthesizes signals across industry, competitive, social, and internal customer data—the same quality of insight a dedicated analyst team would provide, but personalized for each PM's unique product space and delivered automatically every week. This is how data-driven product development should work."*

Product Managers can configure up to 5 competitors per workspace, monitor unlimited industry RSS feeds, connect as many Slack channels and Zendesk tags as needed, and store unlimited historical research for trend analysis. Each workspace operates independently with private data and isolated configurations while sharing the underlying automation infrastructure. PM Radar launched today for all Twilio Product Managers through self-service signup at the internal PM Radar portal, with initial rollout targeting the 5 PMs who validated the prototype and expanding to all product teams following announcement at the next Twilio all-hands and PM team meetings. The platform is built as an open-source internal tool with SD as the initial maintainer, enabling PMs across Twilio to contribute new data source integrations, analysis capabilities, and workspace features as the platform evolves.

## Customer FAQs

**Q: What is being launched today?**

**A:** PM Radar, an internal AI-powered platform that automates product intelligence gathering for Twilio Product Managers. The platform continuously monitors industry trends, competitive moves, social conversations, and internal customer signals, delivering synthesized weekly intelligence digests every Monday morning. Product Managers configure their own workspaces to track competitors, industry sources, Reddit discussions, Zendesk tickets, Gong calls, and Slack VOC channels relevant to their product area.

**Q: Why should I use PM Radar?**

**A:** PM Radar transforms product research from an unsustainable 8-10 hour weekly burden—or infrequent quarterly deep-dives—into a 30-minute Monday morning review. You'll detect competitive launches within days instead of weeks, identify emerging market trends and fraud patterns before they impact customers, correlate social sentiment with internal support data to validate roadmap priorities, and make data-driven decisions backed by comprehensive multi-source intelligence instead of gut feel or anecdotal customer conversations. Early detection is the primary benefit: PM Radar surfaces high-priority signals overnight with detailed investigation memos ready for your Monday morning leadership meetings.

**Q: How and when do I get started?**

**A:** Visit the internal PM Radar portal at pmradar.twilio.com and create a workspace for your product area. Configuration takes 10 minutes: add 2-5 competitors you want to monitor, select industry RSS feeds from the curated list or add custom sources, connect your Zendesk product tags and Slack VOC channels, and choose your Monday morning digest delivery time. Your first weekly digest arrives the following Monday. PM Radar is available now for all Twilio Product Managers.

**Q: Who has access to PM Radar?**

**A:** All Twilio Product Managers have access to PM Radar through self-service signup. Each PM creates their own workspace with private data and isolated configurations. Team leads can share workspace access with other PMs or stakeholders on their team, but insights are not shared across workspaces without explicit permission.

**Q: How does this work if I'm already a Twilio PM?**

**A:** If you're already a Twilio PM, you can create a PM Radar workspace today. Your existing Zendesk, Slack, and Gong access credentials are used to connect internal data sources—no new permissions or approvals needed. The platform leverages your current access to aggregate customer signals you already have visibility into, but surfaces patterns and correlations you wouldn't catch through manual review.

**Q: What existing systems does PM Radar integrate with?**

**A:** PM Radar integrates with Zendesk (support tickets filtered by product tags), Slack (VOC channels like #customer-feedback and product-specific channels), and Gong (sales call transcripts filtered by product area or keywords). For external sources, the platform automatically collects from RSS feeds you configure, monitors competitor websites for changes, and searches Reddit discussions based on your specified keywords and subreddits. All integrations use existing APIs—no custom development required per workspace.

**Q: How does this relate to other Twilio internal tools?**

**A:** PM Radar complements existing tools rather than replacing them. Crayon provides competitive intelligence dashboards; PM Radar extends that with automated monitoring, AI-powered synthesis, and correlation across internal customer signals. Zendesk, Slack, and Gong remain your source systems; PM Radar aggregates signals from them to surface patterns you wouldn't see reviewing each tool individually. The platform feeds insights into your existing workflow—use PM Radar intelligence to inform backlog grooming, roadmap planning, and PDLC artifact creation.

**Q: Does PM Radar use AI?**

**A:** Yes. PM Radar uses AI for three core capabilities: (1) analyzing and summarizing data from all configured sources to identify themes and patterns, (2) detecting anomalies like unusual competitor activity or sentiment spikes that warrant PM attention, and (3) autonomously investigating high-priority signals overnight by scraping competitor pages, searching Gong transcripts, and cross-referencing Slack history to deliver detailed memos by Monday morning. All AI analysis includes source citations so you can verify findings and trace insights back to original data.

**Q: What resources does PM Radar require? What is the operational cost?**

**A:** PM Radar operational costs are covered by Platform Engineering and scale based on workspace usage. A single workspace costs approximately $12-26/month for AI API calls during the MVP phase, increasing to $28-55/month as internal data sources and investigation capabilities are added. At full scale supporting 20 Twilio PM workspaces, total platform cost is estimated at $300-600/month—less than hiring one junior analyst. PMs do not need to budget or expense anything; Platform Engineering absorbs all infrastructure and API costs.

**Q: Which teams or environments is PM Radar available in?**

**A:** PM Radar is available to all Twilio Product Managers globally. The platform runs on Twilio's internal infrastructure and connects to Twilio's Zendesk, Slack, and Gong instances. There are no geographic restrictions or team-specific access requirements. Any Twilio PM can create a workspace and start receiving weekly intelligence digests.

**Q: What new things would I see as part of PM Radar?**

**A:** Every Monday morning, you receive an email with your weekly intelligence digest containing: (1) an executive summary of high-priority signals, source counts, and recommended actions, (2) industry trends synthesized from configured RSS feeds, (3) competitive intelligence summarizing website changes, new features, and pricing updates detected through automated monitoring, (4) social listening insights from Reddit and other platforms showing sentiment trends and discussion volume, (5) internal customer signals aggregating top pain points from Zendesk tickets, feature requests from Slack VOC channels, and themes from Gong sales calls, and (6) investigation memo attachments when high-priority anomalies trigger autonomous deep-dives. You also have access to a web dashboard showing workspace configuration, collection health status, competitor comparison matrices, and searchable historical research archives.

**Q: Is PM Radar standalone or bundled?**

**A:** PM Radar is a standalone internal platform available to all Twilio Product Managers at no charge. It is not bundled with any other Twilio product or service. PMs can use PM Radar independently without adopting other internal tools, though it is designed to complement existing platforms like Crayon, Zendesk, Slack, and Gong by synthesizing insights across them.

**Q: How can I provide feedback on PM Radar?**

**A:** PM Radar is built as an open-source internal platform maintained by SD (Messaging Fraud Prevention PM). Provide feedback through the #pm-radar Slack channel, file issues on the internal GitHub repository, or reach out directly to SD. The platform welcomes contributions: if you have ideas for new data sources, analysis capabilities, or workspace features, you can submit pull requests or work with SD to integrate them.

## Internal FAQs

**Q: Who is the target audience? Who is NOT?**

**A:** The primary target audience is Twilio Product Managers who need continuous intelligence about their product's competitive landscape, customer sentiment, and market trends—especially PMs managing external-facing products where competitive dynamics and customer feedback drive roadmap priorities. SD (Messaging Fraud Prevention PM) represents the archetype: monitoring competitors like Sinch and Vonage, tracking evolving fraud patterns from industry research, correlating Reddit discussions about SMS pumping with internal Zendesk tickets. Secondary audiences include Senior PMs managing multiple products who benefit from aggregated cross-product intelligence, and PM leads who want visibility into market signals across their team's portfolio. PM Radar is NOT designed for PMs managing purely internal tools with no competitive landscape, Engineering Managers focused on technical execution rather than market strategy, or GTM teams who need real-time competitive battle cards (Crayon remains the better fit for sales enablement).

**Q: What do we want people saying about PM Radar?**

**A:** "PM Radar gives me the competitive and customer intelligence I need to make confident roadmap decisions without burning 8-10 hours every week on manual research. I detect competitive launches and customer sentiment shifts within days instead of weeks, and the Monday morning digest with investigation memos has become the most valuable input to my backlog grooming sessions."

**Q: What will PMs like most? Be most disappointed about?**

**A:** PMs will like most the **early detection and time savings**: discovering competitive launches days after they happen instead of weeks later through customer escalations, identifying emerging fraud patterns from industry research before they impact Twilio customers, and spending 30 minutes reviewing synthesized insights instead of 8-10 hours manually tracking sources. The autonomous investigation memos will be particularly valued—waking up Monday morning to a detailed competitive analysis with Gong call cross-references and customer impact assessment, ready to present in leadership meetings. PMs will be most disappointed by **false positive alerts during initial calibration**—high-priority signals that turn out to be noise rather than actionable insights—and **coverage gaps for niche data sources** that aren't supported in the initial MVP (e.g., specialized fraud research platforms beyond RSS feeds, or internal data sources that lack API access). Some PMs may also be disappointed that PM Radar does not replace human judgment: the platform surfaces signals and provides context, but PMs still need to interpret findings and decide on roadmap prioritization.

**Q: Why do we believe PM Radar will be successful?**

**A:** PM Radar success is grounded in validated PM pain and proven automation capabilities. Five Twilio PMs confirmed they spend 8-10 hours weekly on research when they have bandwidth, but realistically conduct deep-dives only quarterly or bi-annually because sustained weekly effort is unsustainable. The prototype validated with the messaging fraud use case demonstrated the automation engine works: RSS collection, Reddit monitoring, competitor discovery, AI-powered analysis, and autonomous investigation all delivered actionable insights in the first weekly digest. The measurable success criteria are adoption (5+ PMs actively using workspaces within 8 weeks), quality (80%+ of insights rated actionable by PMs), and impact (PMs cite PM Radar intelligence in roadmap planning discussions within 12 weeks). Success does not require perfect accuracy—even 70-80% insight quality with occasional false positives delivers more value than quarterly manual research, because continuous monitoring catches time-sensitive signals that quarterly sprints miss entirely.

**Q: Why are we building PM Radar now?**

**A:** Three factors converge to make now the right time: (1) **AI capabilities matured**—autonomous agents that can scrape, search, cross-reference, and synthesize multi-source data became viable in 2024-2026, making the investigation layer feasible where it wasn't 2-3 years ago; (2) **PM research burden increased**—Twilio's product portfolio expanded, competitive landscape intensified with more frequent launches from Sinch/Vonage/MessageBird, and customer expectations for rapid response to market shifts grew, making the 8-10 hour weekly research burden unsustainable; (3) **Internal data infrastructure exists**—Zendesk, Slack, Gong are already instrumented and accessible via APIs, meaning PM Radar can leverage existing sources rather than requiring new data collection infrastructure. Waiting longer means more quarters of PMs operating blind between research sprints, more missed competitive launches, and more roadmap decisions made on incomplete information.

**Q: What durable advantages does PM Radar have for Twilio?**

**A:** PM Radar's durable advantage is the **multi-source synthesis and institutional knowledge accumulation**. While individual components (RSS readers, competitor monitoring, sentiment analysis) are commodities, the platform's value compounds over time: historical research archives enable trend analysis across quarters and years, workspace configurations encode each PM's knowledge of which sources and competitors matter for their product area, and correlation patterns between external signals (Reddit sentiment) and internal data (Zendesk tickets, Gong calls) improve as more data is collected. The open-source internal platform model creates network effects: as more PMs contribute data source integrations and analysis capabilities, every workspace benefits from shared infrastructure improvements. Competitors could replicate individual features, but not the tailored-to-Twilio integration with internal systems or the accumulated historical intelligence specific to Twilio's product portfolio.

**Q: What use cases will PMs NOT want to use PM Radar for?**

**A:** PMs should NOT use PM Radar for: (1) **real-time crisis monitoring**—the weekly digest cadence with Monday morning delivery is designed for strategic planning, not incident response; if you need minute-by-minute social sentiment tracking during an outage, use dedicated monitoring tools; (2) **GTM competitive battle cards**—Crayon remains the better solution for sales enablement with real-time pricing updates and win/loss analysis; PM Radar complements Crayon with broader market context but doesn't replace GTM-focused tools; (3) **customer success account monitoring**—PM Radar aggregates patterns across all customers, not individual account health; CSMs should continue using dedicated customer health platforms; (4) **technical architecture decisions**—PM Radar surfaces market and customer signals to inform product strategy, not infrastructure or technology choices; EMs and architects need different tools for technical due diligence.

**Q: Are we making any one-way door decisions with PM Radar?**

**A:** No significant one-way door decisions. PM Radar is built as an internal open-source platform with no vendor lock-in, no external SaaS contracts, and no proprietary data formats—if the platform were sunset tomorrow, all historical research is stored in markdown and JSON files that PMs can continue referencing. The workspace model with isolated configurations and private data means individual PMs can adopt or abandon PM Radar independently without affecting others. The only semi-permanent decision is the choice to integrate with Twilio's existing internal systems (Zendesk, Slack, Gong) via their APIs, but those integrations are read-only and don't modify source data, so they can be disabled without consequence. The platform's value comes from continuous operation over time (historical trends, pattern detection), so the real "door" is the decision to invest in maintaining the platform long-term—but that commitment scales with adoption and can be adjusted based on measured PM value.

**Q: What is the competitive landscape? What are the alternatives and why is our approach better?**

**A:** The competitive landscape for PM Radar includes both external tools and internal alternatives. **External tools:** Crayon, Klue, and Kompyte provide competitive intelligence dashboards tracking competitor websites, pricing, and news—Twilio already uses Crayon for GTM competitive enablement. However, these tools focus solely on competitive monitoring without integrating internal customer signals (Zendesk, Gong, Slack) or synthesizing across industry research and social listening. They solve one slice of the PM research problem but don't reduce the manual effort required for customer feedback analysis or market trend monitoring. **Internal alternatives:** The status quo is PMs manually reviewing Zendesk tickets, reading Slack VOC channels, searching Gong transcripts, subscribing to industry RSS feeds, and checking competitor websites—spending 8-10 hours weekly when bandwidth allows, or defaulting to quarterly deep-dives when it doesn't. Some teams have experimented with hiring dedicated competitive analysts, but that approach doesn't scale across 20+ product areas and doesn't personalize intelligence to each PM's specific competitors and sources. **Why PM Radar is better:** PM Radar uniquely combines multi-source synthesis (competitive, customer, industry, social) with AI-powered analysis and autonomous investigation, delivering personalized intelligence to each PM's workspace at a fraction of the cost of a dedicated analyst team ($300-600/month for 20 workspaces vs $80K+/year per analyst). The platform's open-source internal model enables Twilio-specific integrations and customization that external SaaS tools cannot provide, while the continuous monitoring cadence catches time-sensitive signals that manual quarterly research misses entirely.

**Q: What are fast-follow features or future plans?**

**A:** Fast-follow features after MVP launch include: (1) **additional data sources**—G2/Capterra/TrustRadius review monitoring beyond the initial RSS and Reddit focus, Twitter/X and LinkedIn social listening, industry conference proceedings and webinar transcripts, patent filings for deep-tech products; (2) **advanced alerting**—real-time Slack notifications for critical signals beyond the weekly digest, customizable alert thresholds per workspace, mobile push notifications for high-priority investigations; (3) **collaborative features**—shared investigation memos across PM teams, workspace templates for common product areas (messaging, voice, auth), cross-workspace trend analysis to identify company-wide competitive threats; (4) **dashboard enhancements**—interactive competitor comparison matrices, visual sentiment trend charts, drill-down from digest summaries into raw source data; (5) **AI capabilities**—predictive trend detection using historical patterns, automated competitive response recommendations, natural language querying of research archives ("show me all fraud pattern discussions from the past 6 months"). The roadmap prioritizes depth over breadth: ensuring the core MVP delivers high-quality insights for the initial 5-10 PMs before expanding to additional sources or advanced features.

**Q: How will we measure success and usage?**

**A:** Success is measured across three dimensions. **Adoption metrics:** Number of active workspaces (target: 5 workspaces in 8 weeks, 10 in 12 weeks, 20 in 24 weeks), weekly active users (PMs who open their Monday digest), workspace configuration completeness (percentage with at least 3 competitors, 10 industry sources, and 2 internal integrations). **Quality metrics:** PM-reported insight actionability via thumbs up/down feedback on each weekly digest (target: 80%+ actionable), false positive rate on high-priority alerts (target: <20%), PM satisfaction survey rating (target: 4+ stars on 5-point scale for "PM Radar improves my ability to make informed roadmap decisions"). **Impact metrics:** Percentage of roadmap planning discussions that cite PM Radar intelligence as an input (tracked via post-meeting surveys), time from competitive launch to Twilio PM awareness (target: <7 days vs historical 14-21 days), PM self-reported time savings on research activities (target: 6+ hours/week reduction). Usage is instrumented through platform telemetry: digest open rates, dashboard page views, investigation memo download counts, search queries on historical archives, workspace configuration changes.

**Q: How do we plan to announce PM Radar internally?**

**A:** PM Radar announcement follows a phased rollout: (1) **Private beta (Weeks 1-4):** The initial 5 PMs who validated the prototype receive access and provide feedback on their first 4 weekly digests; (2) **Twilio PM team announcement (Week 5):** SD presents PM Radar at the monthly PM team meeting, demonstrating the Monday morning workflow with real examples from the beta period, and opening self-service signup to all PMs; (3) **Twilio all-hands mention (Week 6-8):** Brief (~2 minute) all-hands spotlight on PM Radar as an example of bottoms-up innovation and data-driven product development, with signup link shared in Slack immediately after; (4) **#pm-radar Slack channel:** Ongoing updates, tips, and success stories from PMs using the platform. The announcement emphasizes self-service adoption—PMs create workspaces on their own schedule without requiring admin approval—and positions PM Radar as reinforcing PDLC's data-driven approach rather than adding new process overhead.

**Q: What support model is needed for PM Radar?**

**A:** PM Radar operates with a lightweight support model appropriate for an internal open-source platform. **Tier 1 (Self-service):** Comprehensive documentation in the PM Radar GitHub repository covering workspace setup, data source configuration, and common issues; FAQ section addressing typical questions; Slack #pm-radar channel for peer-to-peer support where PMs help each other. **Tier 2 (Maintainer support):** SD as the initial platform maintainer handles bug reports, feature requests, and escalated configuration issues filed as GitHub issues; response SLA is 2 business days for issues affecting multiple workspaces, 1 week for individual workspace configuration help. **Tier 3 (Platform Engineering):** Infrastructure issues affecting platform availability or performance escalate to Platform Engineering team with standard on-call procedures. The support model scales with adoption: as more PMs use the platform and contribute back, the #pm-radar Slack channel becomes the primary support mechanism with distributed knowledge across users, reducing reliance on SD for routine questions. Operational costs (AI APIs, infrastructure) are covered by Platform Engineering budget, not individual PM teams.

**Q: How does PM Radar align with Twilio strategy and the PDLC framework?**

**A:** PM Radar directly reinforces Twilio's PDLC framework requirement for data-driven product development. The PDLC mandates that PR-FAQs and PRDs are grounded in customer evidence, competitive analysis, and market validation—but manually gathering that evidence is time-consuming enough that PMs sometimes shortcut it or rely on anecdotal input. PM Radar solves this by making comprehensive research automatic and continuous: the weekly digest provides the customer signals (Zendesk, Gong, Slack), competitive context (automated competitor monitoring), and market trends (industry RSS feeds, Reddit discussions) that PMs need to write credible PDLC artifacts. The platform also supports Twilio's shift toward proactive rather than reactive product strategy: PMs who detect competitive launches and customer sentiment shifts within days can influence roadmaps before issues escalate, whereas PMs conducting quarterly research only react after the market has already moved. PM Radar is a bottoms-up initiative originating from PM need, but it scales a capability—systematic, data-driven product intelligence—that benefits all product teams and strengthens Twilio's overall product development discipline.
